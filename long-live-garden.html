Case Study

Long Live Garden

Level Design · System Design

Role Game Designer
Focus Level Design, System Design
Year 2026
Platform PC / Browser
01 — Overview

What is Long Live Garden?

Long Live Garden is a cozy puzzle game where players strategically manage plants, tools, and resources to keep a garden alive. The player takes on the role of a caretaker who must balance the health, growth, and survival of multiple plant species across progressively complex levels.

02 — Design Challenge

The Problem

The core challenge was designing a level progression system that introduces mechanics gradually — ensuring players feel competent without the experience becoming trivial. Frustration spikes from poor pacing were a major risk given the game's relaxed tone.

  • How do we introduce complexity without overwhelming casual players?
  • How do we maintain tension in a "cozy" game genre?
  • How do we structure difficulty so it feels earned, not arbitrary?
03 — Design Solution

My Approach

I implemented a structured level progression system built around three pillars: controlled mechanic introduction, graduated difficulty ramping, and deliberate pattern variation to sustain engagement across the full play session.

"Each mechanic was introduced in a safe, low-stakes context before being combined with others — giving players room to experiment without punishment."

04 — Design Process

How I Got There

  • Mapped a level dependency graph to visualize mechanic unlock order
  • Created a difficulty curve structure with explicit "relief" levels after peaks
  • Ran multiple playtesting rounds with target audience profiles
  • Iterated on pacing based on player drop-off data and feedback
  • Balanced challenge and accessibility across skill levels
05 — Result

Outcome

The progression system successfully improved player retention across all tested sessions. The structured difficulty curve created a satisfying sense of mastery, with players completing the full level set at a significantly higher rate than the initial prototype.
