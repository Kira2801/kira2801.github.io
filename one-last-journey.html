Case Study

One Last Journey

Game Design · Economy Design

Role Game Designer
Focus Game Design, Economy Design
Year 2026
Platform Mobile
01 — Overview

What is One Last Journey?

One Last Journey is a narrative-driven mobile game following an aging adventurer on their final quest. The game blends emotional storytelling with lightweight RPG mechanics, requiring a finely tuned economy to ensure player progression feels meaningful without becoming pay-to-win.

02 — Design Challenge

The Problem

Balancing a mobile game economy is notoriously difficult — the system must reward engagement without creating grind loops that alienate players, while still creating meaningful resource decisions throughout the game's arc.

  • How do we create meaningful resource scarcity without frustrating players?
  • How do we balance free-to-play accessibility with optional monetization?
  • How do we tie the economy to the emotional narrative beats of the story?
03 — Design Solution

My Approach

I designed a dual-currency economy with clear separation between earned and premium resources. Soft currency was tied to narrative milestones, ensuring players always had enough to progress the story — while hard currency was reserved for cosmetic and quality-of-life enhancements.

"The economy was designed to make every resource decision feel intentional — players should feel like wise stewards, not resource-starved grinders."

04 — Design Process

How I Got There

  • Mapped full resource flow from session start to end-game
  • Modeled economy simulations across different player archetypes
  • Designed trailer and trailer flow to communicate the emotional arc
  • Balanced reward frequency against narrative pacing
  • Iterated economy values based on closed beta data
05 — Result

Outcome

The economy system achieved a strong balance between engagement and accessibility. Day-7 retention improved significantly in the final beta build, and player feedback consistently highlighted the game's progression as "fair and rewarding."
